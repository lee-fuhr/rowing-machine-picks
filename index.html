How we choose our picks

We don’t run timed lab tests or claim to row a million metres on every machine. Instead, we rely on three things: long-term owner feedback from forums and review sites, published evaluations from respected fitness reviewers, and close attention to what actually breaks (or doesn’t) over years of use. Durability, parts availability, and real-world customer service matter more to us than flashy specs. Every recommendation starts with a simple question: would we buy this machine with our own money? We weigh price against long-term value, and we’re honest about trade-offs, like when a cheaper model compromises on adjustability or a connected rower locks you into a subscription.
