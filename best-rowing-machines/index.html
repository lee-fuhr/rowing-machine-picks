Best for specific needs

Best for long-term reliability and resale value

If you plan to keep your rower for a decade, want easy access to spare parts, and even hope to sell it later for a decent chunk back, the Concept2 RowErg is the only choice. It’s the Toyota Camry of rowing machines: boring to look at, bulletproof to own. No other rower holds its value as well. Our pick: Concept2 RowErg.

Best for small apartments and shared walls

When noise is your biggest concern, magnetic resistance is the way to go. The Hydrow Wave and Fitness Reality 1000 Plus are both near-silent, but the Wave offers a much more polished connected experience. If you can swing the budget, the Wave is the standalone quiet performer that won’t wake anyone up. Our pick: Hydrow Wave.

Best for keeping motivation high without thinking about it

Gamified workouts and auto-resistance take the mental load out of training. The Ergatta’s challenges and the NordicTrack RW900’s iFIT auto-adjust are both very effective at keeping you moving. If you have the budget and love data-driven play, Ergatta wins. If you prefer coached scenic rows, go NordicTrack. Our pick: Ergatta.

Our verdict

There’s no single perfect rowing machine, the best one depends on your space, your budget, and how you like to be motivated. If you want the most reliable, best-supported machine that will still be going strong when the next decade rolls around, the Concept2 RowErg is the honest answer. If you need quiet and connected features on a budget, go with the Fitness Reality 1000 Plus. For a premium, fully immersive experience, the Hydrow Wave and Ergatta each offer something special. Pick the one that fits your life, and you’ll be rowing strong for years.
